About The Singers:

Image Table
Taylor Swift (born December 13, 1989) is an American singer-songwriter, universally recognized as one of the most influential and successful music artists in history. She is the highest-grossing live music artist and the wealthiest female musician, having built a $2 billion empire primarily from composing and performing her own music.
Teddy Vogel is an independent singer and recording artist known for his moody, atmospheric bedroom pop and indie-soul tracks. He has cultivated a dedicated streaming following with viral, emotionally resonant hits like "Late Night Walks," "Obsessed With You," and "Trustfall".
Katy Perry is an American pop superstar renowned for her vibrant aesthetic, empowering anthems, and record-breaking hits. Born Katheryn Elizabeth Hudson on October 25, 1984, she dominated the 2010s music scene with iconic albums like Teenage Dream and Prism.
Ruth B (born Ruth Berhe, July 2, 1995) is a Canadian singer-songwriter best known for her viral, Peter Pan-inspired piano ballad, "Lost Boy." She built a massive global following starting in 2013 by posting short, 6-second singing snippets on the Vine platform.
Rosé (born Roseanne Park) is a New Zealand and South Korean singer, best known as the main vocalist and lead dancer of the globally acclaimed girl group BLACKPINK. Born in Auckland and raised in Australia, she has built a massive international presence in both music and fashion.
Danny O'Donoghue (born October 3, 1980) is an Irish singer, songwriter, and record producer, best known as the frontman of the pop-rock band The Script. He also gained mainstream fame as a vocal coach on the first two series of The Voice UK.
Olivia Rodrigo is an American singer-songwriter and actress born on February 20, 2003, in Murrieta, California. She rose to fame on Disney shows like Bizaardvark and High School Musical: The Musical: The Series before becoming a global pop-punk and alternative-pop sensation with massive, record-breaking albums like Sour (2021) and Guts (2023).
Lewis Capaldi is a multi-platinum Scottish singer-songwriter, born on October 7, 1996, in Glasgow. He rocketed to global fame with his 2018 smash hit "Someone You Loved," which topped charts worldwide. Known for his soulful, raspy voice and self-deprecating humor, he has become one of the UK’s most celebrated modern artists.
TV Girl is an American indie pop band formed in San Diego in 2010 by Brad Petering and Trung Ngo (though the current lineup largely revolves around Petering, Jason Wyman, and Wyatt Harmon). They are best known for blending nostalgic, 1960s French pop and vintage samples with modern hip-hop beats and cynically romantic lyrics.
Billie Eilish Pirate Baird O'Connell is an American singer-songwriter who redefined 2020s pop with her haunting vocals and deeply intimate, minimalist electronic sound. The Los Angeles native skyrocketed to fame at age 14 and is the youngest artist in history to win all four major Grammy Awards in a single year.
Nessa Barrett (born Janesa Jaida Barrett, August 6, 2002) is an American singer-songwriter and social media personality. She rose to fame on TikTok, where she initially shared videos as a teenager, before successfully transitioning into a recording artist with Warner Records.
Melanie Martinez is an American singer-songwriter, director, and visual artist who rose to fame in 2012 on The Voice. She is famous for her unique, conceptual pop music that blends dark, mature themes with whimsical, childhood-inspired aesthetics.
